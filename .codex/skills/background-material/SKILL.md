# Background Material

Use this skill when writing or editing documentation pages, especially `docs/*.md` files with `Learn more` sections.

## Goal

Keep background references neutral, evergreen, and useful for understanding the underlying concept behind Querylight features.

## Acceptable material

- Neutral concept references such as Wikipedia for information retrieval, tries, phrase search, GeoJSON, JSON, or related topics.
- Standards and specifications such as RFCs.
- Primary academic sources such as papers from the IR Anthology or similar reputable venues.
- Platform-neutral documentation for general tooling when it is directly relevant, such as MDN for JavaScript or Vitest docs for testing.
- Existing Querylight repository material when linking within the project is appropriate.

## Not acceptable

- Elasticsearch, OpenSearch, Solr, Algolia, Meilisearch, Typesense, or other competing product documentation in `Learn more` sections.
- Competitor marketing pages, product tutorials, benchmark claims, or feature-comparison content.
- Background links that position Querylight relative to a competing product instead of explaining the core concept itself.

## Editing rules

- If the only available `Learn more` references are competitor links, remove them and leave fewer links. An empty or shorter section is preferable to competitor promotion.
- Prefer 1 to 3 links that explain the concept directly.
- Favor stable references over transient blog posts.
- Keep link text descriptive and neutral.
- Do not add background links just to fill space.

## Review checklist

- Does each link explain the concept rather than a competitor product?
- Would the page still read well if the link stood alone years from now?
- Does the section avoid sending readers to competing search products?
